# Codex Idle TCG - Upgrade Ideas Backlog

## Purpose

This document records the current upgrade and unlock ideas for Codex Idle TCG. It is meant to serve as a design backlog for future content expansion, balancing, and milestone planning.

The upgrade philosophy remains:

- early upgrades reduce frustration
- midgame upgrades improve engine consistency
- lategame upgrades break deckbuilder rules on purpose

## Skill Tree Ideas

- +1 max hand size
- +1 opening hand size
- Always start with a mana card in opening hand
- Reveal top card of deck
- Reveal next 3 cards of deck
- View full deck order

- Increase draw speed

- First mana card each fight triggers twice
- Preserve some mana between monsters

- Generate wild mana passively
- Reduce mana cost of all cards by 1

### Damage and Combat Breakage

## Card Upgrade Ideas

### Baseline Stat Growth

- +base damage
- +base block
- +mana generated
- +cards drawn
- +shield amount
- +overflow amount

### Cost and Efficiency

- -1 mana cost at higher upgrade levels
- Refund mana on play
- Cost reduction if played early in fight
- Cost reduction if matching current mana color streak

### Effect Expansion

- Damage card also draws 1
- Mana card also grants wild mana
- Block card also refunds mana

### Reliability and Flow

- Retain in hand
- Draw replacement on play
- Cantrip behavior added at higher level
- Double-trigger chance
- Improved reshuffle value if exhaust is added later

## Deckbuilder-Breaking Late-Game Ideas

These are the most important upgrades for the long-term fantasy.

- First X cards each monster cost 0
- Draw and play top card automatically
- Every click plays adjacent cards too
- All mana cards trigger twice
- Excess mana becomes damage
- Deck reshuffles instantly with no downtime
- Discarded cards return to hand
- Playing a mana card also attacks
- Playing an attack also draws
- Killing one monster damages all queued monsters
- Card costs can be permanently reduced to 0
- Hand limit becomes irrelevant
- Play cards directly from deck top
- Attacks chain infinitely while kills continue

## Suggested Progression Phases

### Early Game

Focus on smoothing and frustration reduction.

- max hand size
- opening hand size
- start with mana
- mana card in opening hand
- attack flat damage
- reveal top card

### Midgame

Focus on engine consistency and color expression.

- view deck order
- extra mana generation
- mana carryover
- draw automation
- multi-color support
- boss reward improvement
- attack scaling and cheapening

### Late Game

Focus on absurd power-fantasy and illegal-feeling deck behavior.

- free low-cost cards
- multi-play per click
- auto-play systems
- overkill chain damage
- persistent mana economy
- deck order control and self-playing engines
- top-deck autoplay
- cost collapse toward zero

## Notes for Balancing

- early upgrades should make the game smoother, not trivial
- midgame upgrades should create deck identity and engine consistency
- late-game upgrades should intentionally break normal deckbuilder restrictions
- rule-breaking upgrades are more important than plain percentage increases
- persistent upgrades should unlock new play patterns, not only bigger numbers
